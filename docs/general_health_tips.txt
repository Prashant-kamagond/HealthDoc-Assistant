GENERAL HEALTH AND WELLNESS TIPS

Hydration
Adults should drink approximately 8 glasses (about 2 liters) of water per day. Proper hydration supports kidney function, regulates body temperature, and aids digestion. Signs of dehydration include dark urine, dry mouth, fatigue, and dizziness. During exercise or hot weather, increase fluid intake accordingly. Herbal teas and water-rich foods like fruits and vegetables also contribute to daily hydration needs.

Sleep
Adults need 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep per night for optimal health. Poor sleep is linked to increased risk of heart disease, obesity, diabetes, and weakened immune function. Maintain a consistent sleep schedule, keep your bedroom cool and dark, avoid screens one hour before bed, and limit caffeine intake after 2 PM. If you regularly have trouble falling or staying asleep, consult a healthcare provider.

Preventive Care
Regular health screenings and checkups are essential for early detection of disease. Adults should schedule annual physical exams. Recommended screenings include blood pressure checks (every 1-2 years), cholesterol tests (every 4-6 years for low-risk adults), blood glucose tests (starting at age 35), colorectal cancer screening (starting at age 45), and vision and dental exams every 1-2 years. Women should have mammograms starting at age 40-50 and Pap smears every 3 years from age 21.

Immune System Support
Support your immune system through a balanced diet rich in vitamins C, D, and zinc. Get vaccinations as recommended by your healthcare provider. Wash hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption. Managing chronic stress through relaxation techniques also strengthens immune defenses.

Posture and Ergonomics
Maintain good posture to prevent back pain and musculoskeletal issues. When sitting, keep your feet flat on the floor, knees at 90 degrees, and the screen at eye level. Take breaks every 30-60 minutes to stand and stretch. Use a supportive chair and position your keyboard so your elbows are bent at 90 degrees. Poor posture over time can lead to chronic neck pain, headaches, and spinal problems.

Skin Health
Protect your skin by applying broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 30 or higher daily, even on cloudy days. Wear protective clothing and seek shade between 10 AM and 4 PM when UV rays are strongest. Perform regular self-examinations for new or changing moles. See a dermatologist annually for professional skin checks. Stay hydrated and moisturize regularly to maintain the skin barrier.

Oral Health
Brush teeth twice daily with fluoride toothpaste and floss at least once per day. Visit a dentist every six months for cleanings and checkups. Limit sugary foods and acidic beverages that erode enamel. Do not smoke or use tobacco products, as they increase risk of gum disease and oral cancer. Replace your toothbrush every 3-4 months or sooner if bristles are frayed.
